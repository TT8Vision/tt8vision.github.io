The Person Behind The Brand

MEET
NOAH
KERCHHOFF.

"A manufacturer's job isn't to make what's easy — it's to make what's right. To spec, on time, with nothing hidden. That's the standard I hold my own label to, and it's the same standard every client gets."

Noah grew up on the Cape Flats — sketching clothes for figurines before he could afford fabric. That obsession became a discipline. He didn't just design; he learned the entire production chain from the ground up. Fabric weights. Pattern grading. Seam allowances. Branding application. Yield management. The unglamorous details that separate a manufacturer from someone who just orders stock.

IMADETHEARK started as his own label — built without a factory floor, without investors, without shortcuts. That constraint made him precise. Every decision had a cost he personally absorbed: the wrong fabric, a poor cut, a sample that didn't hold. He fixed it himself. That experience is the credential. Not a certificate — a production history.

Today Noah manufactures for schools, corporates, sports clubs, hospitality groups, NGOs, music collectives, and direct-to-consumer brands across South Africa. The product changes. The standard doesn't. You get the same attention he gives his own drops — because to him, there is no difference.

How It Works

FROM BRIEF
TO BRAND-READY.

Five clear steps. No guesswork. No surprises. Just your garment, done right.

01
Share Your Vision

Book a call. Tell Noah what you're building — silhouette, fabric weight, branding, quantity. No idea is too early or too specific. He's heard it all.

02
Design & Source

Noah sources premium fabrics — 180gsm+ carded cotton, twill, fleece, performance blends. A full tech pack is drawn up with your branding, sizing, and construction specs.

03

Before production starts, you see and approve a sample. Fit, finish, colour — everything confirmed before a single unit goes into bulk. No surprises on delivery day.

04

Bulk manufacturing with the same standard as the sample. Double-stitched hems, neck tape, tubular ribbing. Every unit built to the same spec — not approximated at scale.

05
Quality & Deliver

Three quality checks before anything ships. Finished, branded, and packaged to your spec. Whether it's 50 units for a school or 500 for a drop — it leaves right.

Production Standards

HOW IT'S
BUILT.

Manufacturing isn't a product category — it's a set of decisions made at every stage of production. These are the ones that matter.

Raw Material Sourcing

180gsm+ carded cotton, twill, fleece, performance blends, stretch fabrics — sourced to spec, not to price. Every fabric choice is matched to the garment's function: a school uniform needs different durability than a streetwear drop.

Tech Pack Precision

Every order starts with a full technical specification: pattern grading, seam allowances, construction notes, colour references, and branding placement with exact measurements. Nothing is left to interpretation on the factory floor.

Construction Standard

Double-stitched hems and sleeves, tubular neck ribbing with tape reinforcement, overlock seaming throughout. The construction method is chosen to match the end use — not defaulted to the cheapest option.

Branding Application

Screen printing, embroidery, woven labels, heat transfer vinyl, sublimation, rubber patches, hangtags. Applied to exact placement specs with colour-matched inks and threads. Noah has been doing this on his own label — he doesn't guess at positioning.

3-Stage QC Inspection

Quality gates at post-cut (dimensions and yield), post-sew (seam integrity, finish, construction compliance), and pre-pack (branding accuracy, final inspection, count verification). Each stage is documented. Nothing ships that hasn't passed all three.

Direct Communication

You speak to Noah — not a sales team, not a production coordinator, not an account manager. The person who takes your brief is the person who builds your order. Updates, decisions, and problems are handled at source, not filtered through layers.
